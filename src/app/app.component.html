Space is messy. Space, the final frontier, vast void speckled with planets and it’s stars and well… JUNK. You see, space is messy, REALLY MESSY. But why should we care? What is space junk? Why clear space?

The first satellite was launched in 1957 by the USSR as part of their great space race against the USA.

Little Sputnik was the first, but certainly wasn’t the last.

Today, there are over 6000, 6542 to be precise,

and half of them don’t even work anymore.

But that is not all that is floating around out there.

There are spaceships parts, default missiles, even US astronaut Ed Whites’ glove.

With so much floating around up there, could we be in danger?
